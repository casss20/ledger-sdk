Frequently asked questions

A governed action is any call to POST /v1/actions that the Citadel kernel evaluates — regardless of whether it's executed, blocked, or escalated to a human. Dry-run calls also count. Actions that fail at schema validation before reaching the kernel do not count.
We'll email you at 80% and 100% usage. Above the limit, additional actions are billed at $0.0008 per action on Growth and $0.0004 per action on Business — applied automatically with no service interruption. Enterprise customers negotiate overage pricing in their contract.
Yes — VPC and on-premises deployment is available on the Enterprise plan. The kernel runs as a containerized service (Docker/Kubernetes). Audit events can be written to your own database or streamed to your SIEM. Contact us for architecture details.
Citadel provides first-class SDK integrations for LangGraph, Claude Code, Codex, and K2.6. Any framework that can make HTTP calls to POST /v1/actions works with Citadel — the REST API is framework-agnostic. Additional native integrations (LangChain, CrewAI, AutoGen) are in development.
PROBATION (score 0.20–0.39) means the agent can still submit actions, but all of them are routed to the human approval queue — nothing executes autonomously. The default probation duration is 7 days, minimum 3. REVOKED (score 0.00–0.19) means all submissions are blocked outright by the kernel. An agent exits PROBATION automatically when its score crosses 0.40.
Each audit event has an event_hash (SHA-256 of the event's own content) and a prev_hash linking it to the previous event. This creates a chain where any deletion or modification makes the hashes invalid. You can verify the entire chain at any time via GET /v1/audit/verify — it returns which event broke the chain if tampering is detected.
