Core Architecture

Three pillars, one kernel

Every agent action flows through a single decision point. No partial coverage, no sampling — full evaluation on every call.

01

Visible

Complete, tamper-evident audit trails for every decision the kernel makes. Every action carries a cryptographic chain back to its root.

  • Every action decision logged with event hash
  • Immutable prev_hash chain — tamper detection built in
  • Lineage tracking: root → parent → child decisions
  • GET /v1/audit/verify returns chain integrity
02

Controlled

Policy evaluation happens before any action executes. Kill switches, capability tokens, and human approval gates give you layered control at every scope.

  • Rule-based policies with 6 scope types
  • Kill switches at request, agent, tenant, or global scope
  • Capability tokens gate high-risk action categories
  • Human approval queues with SLA and priority
03

Compliant

Map any policy directly to a regulatory framework. Citadel generates compliance evidence at the action level — not reconstructed from logs after the fact.

  • 7 frameworks: SOC2, ISO27001, NIST, GDPR, HIPAA, PCI_DSS, CUSTOM
  • Per-decision framework citation in audit events
  • Exportable compliance reports by time range
  • EU AI Act Art. 9, 14, 17 alignment built in

Kill Switches

One call. Four scopes. Instant halt.

When an agent goes off-script, you don't have a minute to wait. Citadel's kill switches stop execution at the scope you choose — a single request, a specific agent, your entire tenant, or globally across all deployments.

Trust Band System

Dynamic trust — not static roles

Agent trust scores are computed from seven behavioral factors and mapped to one of five immutable bands. Bands determine which actions an agent can take without approval, which require human review, and which are blocked outright.

A new agent starts at STANDARD (0.40). It earns TRUSTED or HIGHLY_TRUSTED through verification, time, and behavioral health — and drops to PROBATION or REVOKED if compliance signals deteriorate.
